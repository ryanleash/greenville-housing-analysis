Key Insights

The Upstate SC Industrial Boom

Between 2017 and 2025, the Greenville-Spartanburg corridor attracted over $7 billion in announced industrial investment — led by BMW’s $1.7B EV pivot, Scout Motors’ $2B greenfield plant, and Boeing’s $1B Dreamliner expansion.

These announcements correlate strongly with Greenville County’s population surge (527K → 574K from 2020–2024) and home value appreciation across the metro. Travelers Rest, positioned on the Swamp Rabbit Trail at the base of the Blue Ridge foothills, captured outsized demand from in-migrating professionals — its population grew 70% from 2010–2020.

The 2022 rate shock (mortgage rates jumping from 3.2% to 6.7%) froze inventory as existing homeowners refused to sell, compressing supply. Median sale prices in TR plateaued but did not meaningfully correct — a sign of genuine demand, not speculation.

Mortgage delinquencies remain near historic lows (1.78%), suggesting the Upstate’s price appreciation is backed by real income growth, not over-leveraged buyers.
